What we mean by agile governance

Delivering change is, currently steered from within functional silos which come with functional governance groups. When a programme of change is initiated it is often hindered and slowed down by the amount of governance groups it has to report into, with no one group having the ultimate control and say.

Supporting the service approach, the service governance group is made up from a multi-disciplinary (multi-functional ) team but whom report and are guided by the Service Owner (and a Service Executive). This approach will streamline decision making, put accountability and ownership in the right place and enable the service to deliver with agility and flexibility.

The activities and outcomes

The Service Governance group will:

  • Support the delivery of the service vision and roadmap (the outcomes)
  • Understand the user needs and the pain points
  • Make decisions and clear escalated blockers
  • Own and manage the risks
  • Own and manage dependencies, for example people, process, policy as well as technology common platforms
  • Own the benefits of the service

What is required

A move from functional governance to service governance - where the focus is on how we deliver the outcomes and the user need.

Adopting a team onion approach to governance will enable the governance group to manage the core team, the collaborators and the supporters

What are the benefits

Less but more targeted governance enables:

  • Faster decisions
  • Quicker actions
  • Earlier benefits realised
