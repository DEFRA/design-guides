What we mean by service design

Service design is the strategic design of a service around its users’ needs and business process. Service design means getting an in-depth understanding of a service area and its users, looking at things from an end-to-end perspective and designing it ‘from the bottom up’. To a citizen, services are things a Government provides that enables them to complete a selection of tasks so they can achieve their goal. A service must meet its users’ needs in order to deliver on policy intent.

The activities and outcomes

  • Define the problem statement
  • Collaborate with Policy colleagues to understand policy outcomes
  • Define services around how a user would understand or engage with them
  • Define sub-services to understand all tasks a user needs to complete to achieve their goal
  • Map all channels, including digital, face-to-face, phone or post in the context of a real user journey
  • Identify opportunities for big impact, like call volumes or long duration of transactions
  • Develop prototypes that can be tested with users iteratively so solutions are based on evidence not assumptions
  • Map capabilities in a service to provide the best solution for users
  • Identify common design and service patterns so users get a consistent experience on GOV.UK
  • Visualise and design for collaboration and shared understanding
  • Simplify and clarify process and language so anyone can understand it

What is required

Co-design facilitation with groups including:

  • Policy teams
  • Strategy and business change teams
  • Delivery portfolio teams
  • Delivery teams
  • Content teams
  • Operational staff

What are the benefits

  • Building services that meet practical and emotive user needs
  • Enabling policy outcomes to be delivered through task completion
  • Designing services that meet the Government Service Standard
  • Reduction of cost and time by simplifying services, so users succeed first time
  • End-to-end design of user-facing services, internal processes, supporting policy or legislation and organisational, financial and governance structures
  • Creating common patterns which can be simplified, standardised and shared so they can reused or scaled across organisations

The service design process

  • Apply design thinking and design strategies
  • Apply GDS Design principles
  • Developing practical service blueprints to show a whole ecosystem
  • Stepping back and seeing the big picture in a holistic way
  • Collaboration with the cross Government User Centred Design community
