Why we need a service lifecycle

To support the opportunities of digital technologies and agile working practices, we need to re-focus how we deliver change. We need to move away from the traditional ‘project’ approach, where we come up with an idea a solution, we add it to a list, the list is prioritised with everything else on the list and then we seek funding and people to deliver the idea or solution. Technology is moving at a fast pace and is providing different ways of interacting with customers, delivering their needs and collecting data.

What it means

We are organising ourselves around what the user needs to do, for example, going fishing, manage waste, use water or keep animals.

We are understanding the policy outcomes and the reason why we ask the user to do what we ask them to do.

We are creating multi-disciplinary teams that support the different lens or view of the service.

We frame problems around a service so we can:

  • Simplify
  • Cut down on governance
  • Stop doing things we don't need to do

Benefits of having a service lifecycle:

  • Reduced start up costs
  • Maximising knowledge of the service team
  • Respond to changing priorities to maximise value for us and the end user
  • Collaborative working creates opportunities to be innovative and reduce waiting times
  • Business and technology changes are a continuum
  • Deliver small changes with big value
  • Supporting the macro economic view

About this prototype

The Service Lifecycle is NOT a replacement of the GDS Service Design Manual and will make links to existing guidance where possible.
